[conversational tone] About pages are strange things to write. [reflective] They ask you to describe yourself like a stranger, and I have always been suspicious of that.

[matter-of-fact] So, plainly.

[gently] I am Boris.

[matter-of-fact] I have been writing software for a bit over fourteen years. The work pays the rent, feeds the dogs, and gives me a problem to chew on most mornings. These days I am the backend half of a small product team, somewhere between Laravel, Docker, and Kubernetes. Most of my worries are queues that should drain faster than they fill, and indexes that grow faster than I would like.

[reflective] Before software, I studied mathematics at university. I never quite stopped. Mathematics is the place I go when the world gets too loud. Number theory especially. There is something honest about a prime number that I do not find in many other places.

[conversational tone] A lot of what I write here starts from that direction.

A small mathematical itch. A half-understood medical paper. A French sentence I had to read four times. Then I pull on it and see what else is attached.

[gently] The blog is where the pulling happens.

[matter-of-fact] None of it is expert work. I read medicine I have no business reading. I read Bulgarian and French novels and pretend I am keeping up. I am learning German slowly, mostly because the grammar amuses me. I write C plus plus by hand sometimes. Not for work. Just because the discipline is its own thing.

[reflective] The essays come out of all that. Quiet attempts to write down what I noticed before I forget it.

[gently] I live in Sofia. Two rescued strays, Kozhuhka and Ekler, make sure I do not take myself too seriously. They have failed before. They keep trying.

[reflective] That is most of it.

[matter-of-fact] There is a small freelance shingle I keep up under the name Percepticus, for work that arrives by word of mouth. Otherwise the essays are here, and I am content to be quiet about the rest.
