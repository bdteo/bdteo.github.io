Intimate documentary still life cover image for an essay asking whether human connection could be treated as public infrastructure, in the same visual direction as a quiet public bench with two cups, but warmer and more wholesome.

A clean public wooden bench at early golden morning in a small city square, close and human, with generous empty space above. On the lower-right bench slats sits a folded charcoal wool scarf softened by sunlight. Near the center are two plain ivory ceramic cups, one slightly closer to the viewer, both unbranded and clean. Between them is a small linen napkin holding two simple round homemade biscuits, no packaging, no plate decoration. The cups and biscuits feel like someone made space for another person, without sentimentality. In the background, a soft blur of a public library doorway and pale paving stones, no signs or readable text. The hero is a public place becoming gently hospitable.

Shot on Leica M6 with 35 mm Summicron, Kodak Portra 400 film, shallow depth of field, natural film grain, soft halation on the cup rims and bench varnish.
